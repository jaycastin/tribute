Tribute to Dr. Carlos J. Finlay

Carlos J. Finlay was a Cuban epidemiologist recognized as a pioneer in the research of yellow fever, determining that it was transmitted through mosquitoes

  • First to theorize, in 1881, that a mosquito was a carrier of the organism causing yellow fever.
  • His theory was followed by the recommendation to control the mosquito popuplation as a way to control the spread of the disease.
  • His hypothesis and exhaustive proofs were confirmed nearly twenty years later by the Walter Reed Commission of 1900.
